# D’Zilva’s Addition to Ashby’s Law of Requisite Variety

**Extending Classical Cybernetics to Account for Irreversibility and Suppression in Complex Adaptive Systems**  
Nicholas D’Zilva | Invariant Systems | March 2026

---

## Overview

This repository contains the formal paper:

**"D’Zilva’s Addition to Ashby’s Law of Requisite Variety"**

The work extends the classical formulation by 0 to account for two structural dynamics present in modern complex systems:

- **ΔI(t)** — Irreversibility accumulation (reversibility debt over time)  
- **S(t)** — Suppression load (constraints on effective variety at the framing layer)

The extended condition is defined as:V(controller, t) ≥ V(disturbance, t) + ΔI(t) + S(t)

---

## Core Insight

A system can satisfy the classical Ashby condition and still fail if:

- it is **losing future optionality through irreversible decisions**, or  
- it is **structurally constrained from accessing its full response space**

This work reframes regulation from a **static variety-matching problem** to a **dynamic variety-preservation problem**.
